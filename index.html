Hello! My name is Ya-Sin Luo(it is also written as 羅雅馨 in Chinese). I’m a fourth-year undergraduate student at the National Cheng Kung University. I am majoring in Computer Science and Information.

During my undergraduate education, I worked with Dr. Jenn-Jier Lien in the Computer Science Department to develop a human-robot interaction project and Dr. Hung-Yu Kao to improve the maintainability and usability of a website project.

After my undergrad, I plan to attend graduate school and continue doing research in computer science. I’m interested in how computer vision reaches human-level reasoning, how people integrate theory and practice into applications in self-driving vehicles, and how to utilize virtual/augmented reality to test and improve products. During my leisure time, I enjoy hiking, dancing, and writing both Chinese and English calligraphy.

Research Projects

My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision, cognition, and human-computer interaction. My researches focus on applying deep learning techniques to process visual signal, integrating systems, and designing and implement an automatic procedure.

Research

Robotic Eye-In-Hand Camera for Select-and-Place Tasks

Robotics industrial automation benefits production. Many manufacturers are using different forms of automation to get higher quality and efficiency. However, industrial robots are highly repeatable but not accurate, therefore, we are motivated to use eye-in-hand robotic arms to implement a well-designed automatic procedure.
The objectives include robot arm calibration, object detection, digit recognition, and system integration.
