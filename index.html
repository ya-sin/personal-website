Hello! My name is Ya-Sin Luo(it is also written as 羅雅馨 in Chinese). I'm a graduate student studying EECS at the University of California, Berkeley.

During my undergraduate education, I worked with Dr. Jenn-Jier Lien in the Computer Science Department to develop a human-robot interaction project and Dr. Hung-Yu Kao to improve the maintainability and usability of a website project.

After my undergrad, I had about 1-year of work experience in embedded Linux. I worked on Baseboard Management Controller(BMC) firmware for the edge server.

And now, I'm in UC-Berkeley for my EECS graduate degree and will graduate in May 2022.

I’m interested in how computer vision reaches human-level reasoning, how people integrate theory and practice into applications in self-driving vehicles, and how to utilize virtual/augmented reality to test and improve products. During my leisure time, I enjoy hiking, dancing, and writing both Chinese and English calligraphy.

Research

Robotic Eye-In-Hand Camera for Select-and-Place Tasks

Robotics industrial automation benefits production. Many manufacturers are using different forms of automation to get higher quality and efficiency. However, industrial robots are highly repeatable but not accurate, therefore, we are motivated to use eye-in-hand robotic arms to implement a well-designed automatic procedure.
The objectives include robot arm calibration, object detection, digit recognition, and system integration.
